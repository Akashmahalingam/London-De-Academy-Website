Overview

Nowadays, the hospitality and tourism industries are growing in every corner of the world. People are traveling countries to enjoy life and learning a new culture. Business organisations are looking for skilled and creative business managers to manage the complexes of these industries. The study of this course will help an individual to acquire a formal degree and education for making a career in the hospitality industry. It will make students business professionals according to the demand of the industry.

The hospitality industry has great potential to achieve high-level positions in the desired international market. The presence of diversity in this industry gives a limitless job to students. Students could select any related field such as hotel management, running the remote resort, managing guest houses, etc. according to their preferred choice for making a career in this vast industry.

Entry Requirements: Entry to the qualification will be through interview and learners will be expected to demonstrate some ability and possess qualifications at Level 3, for example, A Levels or vocational awards. Learners may spend some time in an organisational role and shown they have the capability and drive to develop Tourism and Hospitality Management skills.

English Language Requirements: If a learner is not from a majority English-speaking country must provide evidence of English language competency.

Key Information


UK University Study Pathways: Completion of Level 4 and Level 5 Diploma in Tourism and Hospitality Management will meet the university's standard academic entry requirements of an undergraduate final year (top-up) degree. However, each applicant will be subject to individual assessment and other entry requirements which may affect university entry. London De Academy only provides study pathways to progress directly in the final year of an undergraduate UK degree. Please note that the degree is delivered and awarded by the UK university. Feel free to contact our admissions team to discuss the university progression routes and admission requirements


About Ofqual: The Office of Qualifications and Examinations Regulation (Ofqual) regulates qualifications, examinations and assessments in England. A non-ministerial government department with jurisdiction in England. Ofqual is independent of government and report directly to Parliament. Any approved Level 4 and Level 5 qualifications from Ofqual are equivalent to Higher National Certificates (HNC) and Higher National Diploma (HND) or Year 1 and Year 2 of a three-year UK Bachelor's degree programme.

Accreditations: OTHM is a global provider in awarding British qualifications. It's established and recognised Awarding Organisation (Certification Body) launched in 2003 as a not-for-profit organisation. Tens of thousands of learners have attained qualifications and gained advance entry into UK Degree final year and Masters top-up programmes.
